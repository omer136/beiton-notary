# Notarial Power of Attorney in Israel — Types, Requirements & Fees (2026)

A notarial power of attorney (POA) is a legal document in which one person (the "principal") authorizes another (the "agent") to act on their behalf before banks, government agencies, the Land Registry (Tabu), and other bodies. Unlike a regular POA, a power of attorney signed before a notary constitutes **sufficient evidence** without the need for additional proof, as established by Section 19 of the Notaries Law, 1976 (חוק הנוטריונים, תשל"ו-1976).

In this guide, you will learn about the different types of notarial POA, when you need one, how much it costs, and how to obtain one — including through a digital process.

## Types of Notarial Power of Attorney

### General Power of Attorney

Authorizes the agent to perform a wide range of actions on behalf of the principal — managing bank accounts, handling tax matters, signing documents, and more. Ideal for individuals living abroad who need someone to manage their affairs in Israel.

### Real Estate Power of Attorney (Tabu)

A specific POA for real estate transactions — buying, selling, registering rights, or taking out a mortgage. The Land Registry offices (Tabu) accept **only** notarized powers of attorney. A regular POA will be rejected.

### Irrevocable Power of Attorney

A POA that cannot be revoked without the consent of the agent. Common in real estate transactions — for example, a seller signs an irrevocable POA in favor of the buyer to ensure the transaction is registered even if the seller changes their mind.

### Power of Attorney for Banking & Mortgages

Banks require a notarial POA for actions such as adding or removing authorized signatories, managing an account on behalf of someone who cannot visit the branch, or signing mortgage documents. The wording is usually dictated by the bank in advance.

### Power of Attorney for Use Abroad

A POA intended for presentation to authorities abroad. In many cases, an [apostille](/blog/apostille-israel) is also required — an international certification proving the Israeli notary is authorized to act.

## When Is a Notarial Power of Attorney Required?

Not every POA needs to be notarized. Here are the situations where notarization is **mandatory**:

- **Real estate transactions** — any action at the Tabu on behalf of another person
- **Banking transactions** — most banks require a notarized POA
- **Proceedings involving foreign entities** — embassies, foreign authorities, courts abroad
- **Irrevocable POA** — notarization is required by law
- **When the receiving body demands it** — insurance companies, pension funds, etc.

## Notarial Power of Attorney Fees in Israel (2026)

Fees are set by the Notaries Regulations (Service Fees), 1978 (תקנות הנוטריונים (שכר שירותים), תשל"ט-1978) and are uniform nationwide.

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| First signatory on the POA | 197 ₪ |
| Each additional signatory | 77 ₪ |
| Additional certified copy | 77 ₪ |
| Revocation of POA — notice and registration | 240 ₪ |
| Certified copy with revocation note | 85 ₪ |
| Foreign language surcharge (other than Hebrew/English/Arabic) | 104 ₪ |

All prices are **before VAT (18%)**. Fees are uniform as regulated by the Ministry of Justice.

**Example:** POA with two signatories + additional copy = 197 + 77 + 77 = **351 ₪ + VAT = 414.18 ₪**

## Required Documents

To obtain a notarial power of attorney, bring the following:

1. **Valid ID** of the principal (the person granting the POA) — Israeli ID card with supplement, or passport
2. **Agent's details** — full name and ID number
3. **POA wording** — if the receiving body requires a specific format (e.g., a bank or the Tabu)
4. **Land Registry extract** — if the POA relates to real estate
5. **Passport** — if the POA is intended for use abroad

The agent does not need to be present at the signing.

## Notarial Power of Attorney for Use Abroad — Apostille

If the POA is intended for use in a country that is a member of the Hague Convention (1961), you will also need an [apostille](/blog/apostille-israel). The apostille certifies that the Israeli notary is authorized, and that the document is valid.

Not all countries require an apostille — check in advance with the receiving body abroad.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Question: How much does a notarial power of attorney cost in Israel?**
Answer: 197 ₪ before VAT for the first signatory, 77 ₪ for each additional signatory. The fee is uniform nationwide and set by the Notaries Regulations (Service Fees), 1978 (תקנות הנוטריונים).

**Question: Does the agent need to be present at the notary's office?**
Answer: No. Only the principal (the person granting the POA) must appear before the notary and sign. The agent does not need to be present.

**Question: What is the difference between a regular and a notarial power of attorney?**
Answer: A notarial POA is authenticated by a notary and constitutes sufficient evidence under the law. A regular POA may not be accepted by banks, the Land Registry, or foreign entities.

**Question: How long is a notarial power of attorney valid?**
Answer: A notarial POA remains valid as long as it has not been revoked, unless an expiration date is specified in the document. An irrevocable POA cannot be canceled without the agent's consent.

**Question: Can a notarial power of attorney be revoked?**
Answer: Yes, except for an irrevocable POA. Revocation is done by notifying the notary (240 ₪) and it is advisable to also notify the agent and relevant parties directly.

**Question: Do I need an apostille on a POA for use abroad?**
Answer: In most cases, yes — if the destination country is a member of the Hague Convention. The apostille can be obtained through the courts or the Ministry of Justice. Read more in our [apostille guide](/blog/apostille-israel).
