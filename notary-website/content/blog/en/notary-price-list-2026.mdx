# Notary Fees in Israel 2026 — Official Ministry of Justice Tariffs

Looking for up-to-date notary fees in Israel? Notary tariffs are set by the Notaries Regulations (Service Fees), 1978 (תקנות הנוטריונים (שכר שירותים), תשל״ט-1978) and are updated every January 1st according to the Consumer Price Index. Fees are uniform across the country — no notary may charge more or less than the regulated tariff.

## Complete Notary Fee Schedule — 2026

All prices below are **before VAT (18%)**. Tariffs are uniform as set by the Ministry of Justice.

### Signature Authentication (Item 1)

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| First signer on a document | 197 ₪ |
| Each additional signer | 77 ₪ |
| Authorization on behalf of another — per signature | 77 ₪ |
| Additional copy (at time of authentication) | 77 ₪ |

Notarial signature authentication is required when a third party — a bank, government authority, or foreign entity — requires proof that a person signed a document of their own free will. The notary verifies the signer's identity via ID card and confirms the signature was made in their presence.

**Example:** Authentication of two signers on an agreement = 197 + 77 = **274 ₪ + VAT = 323.32 ₪**

### Certified True Copy (Item 2)

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| First page | 77 ₪ |
| Each additional page | 13 ₪ |
| Additional copy — first page | 27 ₪ |
| Additional copy — each additional page | 5 ₪ |

A certified true copy is needed when you must present an official copy of an original document — such as a birth certificate, diploma, or license. The notary examines the original and certifies the copy is identical.

### Certified Translation (Item 3)

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| First 100 words | 251 ₪ |
| Each additional 100 words (up to 1,000) | 197 ₪ |
| Each additional 100 words (over 1,000) | 99 ₪ |
| Additional copy of translation certificate | 77 ₪ |

If the notary translates the document personally (rather than certifying an external translator's work), a **50% surcharge** is added to the certification fee.

## Special Surcharges

### Foreign Language (Item 8)

If the notarial certificate is prepared in a language other than Hebrew, English, or Arabic, an additional **104 ₪** is added to the fee.

### Out-of-Office Service (Item 9)

| Service | Fee |
|---------|-----|
| First hour (including travel) | 645 ₪ |
| Each additional half hour | 197 ₪ |

Plus actual travel expenses. Service between 19:00-08:00 or on rest days — **50% surcharge** on the fixed fee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I negotiate a discount with a notary?**
A: No. Fees are fixed by the Notaries Regulations (Service Fees) and notaries are not permitted to charge more or less than the regulated tariff — deviating from the tariff is a disciplinary offense under Section 42(3) of the Notaries Law.

**Q: When are notary fees updated?**
A: Under Section 4 of the Regulations, fees are updated on January 1st each year according to the Consumer Price Index published by the Central Bureau of Statistics.

**Q: Do the prices include VAT?**
A: No. All prices in the tables above are before VAT. You must add 18% VAT as required by law. For example, signature authentication costs 197 ₪ + 35.46 ₪ VAT = 232.46 ₪ total.

**Q: How much does a notarial translation of a birth certificate cost?**
A: An average birth certificate is approximately 200 words. Translation certification cost: 448 ₪. If the notary translates personally — 672 ₪ + VAT. Calculate your exact price using our [price calculator](https://notary.beiton.co/en).

**Q: Do notary fees differ between notaries?**
A: No. The tariff is identical everywhere in Israel — whether in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa, or Be'er Sheva. The difference lies in level of service, speed, and accessibility.
